Baseline Assessments

Baseline surveys also known as Formative assessments provide quantitative and qualitative information on the current status of a particular situation in a given population. Baseline assessments aim at quantifying the distribution of certain variables in a study population at one point in time therefore benchmark the project indicators.


Baseline Assessments

This is also commonly referred to as the mid-term reviews. At Reinit Research, we endeavor to establish whether a project is heading towards the set goals and objectives, thereafter informing management and control decisions by the project management.

End of Term Evaluations

This evaluation type is also known as the Summative evaluation, Endline evaluation or the Project Completion evaluation. At Reinit Research, we establish project outputs and immediate outcomes, with results of the evaluation compared to the results at baseline and midline. We use the evaluation findings to inform implementors and stakeholders on the project success and is important for documenting success stories and lessons learnt.

As times are changing so are we. Reinit Research has adopted electronic data collection methods that enable online and offline data gathering. Our electronic data collection and analysis capacities allow prompt trend analysis and triangulation while still in the field.
Electronic data collection has the following advantages:

  • Its fast: Use of electronic tools increases the speed of data processing since interviewers carry out multiple tasks of interviewing, data entry and coding concurrently.
  • Its accurate: No requirement for scrutiny or validation programmes since the electronic tool is programmed to avoid multiple errors through preset skip patterns, avoiding missed questions, use of GPS to take interview locations.
  • It cuts cost: Use will cut in fieldwork and analysis time.
  • Quality: It also improves data quality!
